Banners look similar to Call-Outs but serve a different use case. They are usually contextual to the product or task. If you are looking for a way to communicate a global message, like “you are offline” consider using a Toast.

Below is handy table to help you choose when to pick which pattern.

Don’t use colors to signify the intensity of a communication. Instead use banners to signify type of information being communicated.

Try to limit banner usage so that there’s only 1 banner being displayed at a time on screen.

The blue note banners mean “this is something that may be nice to know”. Banners can be dismiss-able (X on the top right), or they may be persistent (no X on the top right). Consider your use case and whether or not it warrants a dismiss-able banner.

<Banner variant="info" dismissible>
  This is an example of a note banner. It spans a single line.
</Banner>

<br />

<Banner variant="info">
  This is an example of a note banner. It spans a single line.
</Banner>

The yellow warning banner means “this might be important so pay attention”.

<Banner variant="warning" dismissible>
  This is an example of a warning banner. It spans a single line.
</Banner>

The danger red banner mean “something is broken, or actively going wrong”.

<Banner variant="danger" dismissible>
  We have detected a potential problem while deploying.
</Banner>

The success green banner should be used sparingly and are used to indicate success, or confirmation. When using a green banner, consider using toasts instead.

<Banner variant="success" dismissible>
  Your cluster has finished provisioning!
</Banner>

We recommend not including CTAs within banners. However, if your specific use-case
calls for it we suggest using the default color button in the extra-small size and
not having any other buttons in the banner.
